# Exercise 005 - Mentorship & Growing Your Team

### Objective
Scale yourself through others. Master the art of **Mentorship** and **Sponsorship**. Learn how to identify the strengths and weaknesses of your teammates, provide constructive (and kind) feedback, and delegate challenging tasks that help junior and senior engineers reach the next level of their careers.

### Core Concepts to Master
- **Mentorship:** Teaching an engineer *how* to solve a problem.
- **Sponsorship:** Using your influence to give an engineer an opportunity (e.g., "I think Sarah should lead the next RFC").
- **The "Socratic" Method:** Asking questions to lead someone to an answer instead of just giving it to them.
- **Constructive Feedback:** Using the "SBI" model (Situation, Behavior, Impact) to provide feedback that leads to change without causing defensiveness.
- **Delegation:** Trusting others to do the work, even if they do it differently than you would.

### The Practical Challenge

**Part 1: The Code Review Mentor**
1. Read a PR from a junior engineer that is full of "Anti-patterns" (e.g., long functions, bad naming).
2. **Bad Review:** "This is messy. Fix it."
3. **Mentor Review:** "I see you used a nested loop here. Research the 'Guard Clause' pattern. How might that make this function easier to read for the next person?"
4. Practice writing 3 mentor-style comments on a hypothetical PR.

**Part 2: Planning a "Growth Opportunity"**
1. You have a "Mid-level" engineer who wants to become "Senior."
2. They are great at coding but afraid of talking to stakeholders.
3. Design a task for them that stretches this specific skill (e.g., "Asking them to demo the new feature to the Design team").

**Part 3: The "Feedback" Conversation**
1. An engineer on your team is consistently late with their work and doesn't communicate when they are stuck.
2. Use the **SBI Model** to prepare for a 1-on-1:
   - **Situation:** "During the last sprint..."
   - **Behavior:** "...you didn't update the Jira tickets for 3 days and missed the Friday deadline..."
   - **Impact:** "...which meant the QA team had to work late on Sunday to catch up."
3. Discuss how to turn this into a "How can I help you?" conversation.

**Part 4: Managing Up**
1. Research how to "Mentor your boss." 
2. If your manager doesn't understand the complex technical problem the team is facing, how do you provide them with the information they need to support you?

### Common Gotchas & Troubleshooting
- **Gotcha 1:** Micro-management. If you delegate a task but then check in every 10 minutes to "Correct" the work, you aren't delegating—you are just using someone else's hands to type. 
- **Gotcha 2:** Only mentoring "People like you." Be aware of your biases. Ensure you are providing mentorship and sponsorship to everyone on the team, regardless of their background or personality.
