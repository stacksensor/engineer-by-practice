# Exercise 001 - The Role of a Tech Lead

### Objective
Understand the shift from "Coding" to "Leverage." Explore what it means to be a **Technical Leader** (Tech Lead, Staff Engineer, or Architect). Learn why your success is no longer measured by your individual lines of code, but by the productivity, quality, and architectural health of the entire team.

### Core Concepts to Master
- **The "Force Multiplier":** A leader who makes everyone around them twice as effective.
- **Staff+ Levels:** Understanding the different tracks (Individual Contributor vs Manager) and the roles of Staff, Principal, and Distinguished engineers.
- **Technical Vision:** The ability to look 6-12 months into the future and align the team toward a shared technical goal.
- **Breadth vs. Depth:** Transitioning from being the master of one specific tool to understanding how all tools in the company fit together.
- **Navigating Ambiguity:** Taking a vague business goal (e.g., "Make the app faster") and turning it into a concrete technical plan.

### The Practical Challenge

**Part 1: Defining Success**
1. Compare two engineers:
   - **Engineer A:** Writes 1,000 lines of perfect code per day in a silo.
   - **Engineer B:** Writes 100 lines of code, but spends the day reviewing 10 PRs, mentoring a junior, and helping the Product Manager avoid a 2-month delay by suggesting a simpler technical solution.
2. Discuss: Who is providing more "Value" to the company at a Senior/Staff level?

**Part 2: The Time Audit**
1. Imagine you are a Tech Lead.
2. Create a "Ideal Week" calendar. 
3. Include time for: Deep work (coding), RFC writing, One-on-Ones, Architectural reviews, and "Uninterrupted block for helping others." 
4. How much time should actually be spent "writing code" in a terminal?

**Part 3: Handling a Technical Dispute**
1. Two senior engineers disagree on which database to use (SQL vs NoSQL). Both are right in different ways.
2. As the Tech Lead, how do you facilitate a decision without making one person feel "defeated"?
3. Research the concept of "Disagree and Commit."

**Part 4: Identifying "Technical Debt"**
1. Research what "Tech Debt" actually is. 
2. Write a short proposal for your manager explaining why the team should spend the next 2 weeks fixing the CI/CD pipeline instead of building a new feature. (Hint: Focus on the "Cost of Inaction").

---

### Common Gotchas & Troubleshooting
- **Gotcha 1:** The "Hero" Complex. A Tech Lead who stays up all night "Fixing everything themselves" is actually failing to grow the team. If the Tech Lead leaves, the system collapses.
- **Gotcha 2:** Meeting Overload. It's easy to spend 40 hours a week in meetings and lose touch with the code. You must maintain enough technical "Depth" to remain a credible leader.
